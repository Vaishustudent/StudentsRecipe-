Story :

I used to help my mom every time I saw her make Jalebi because I loved making it and the process is super fun. Jalebi is really delicious!


Ingredients :

  • Vegetable Oil - 1 Liter
  • Caster Sugar - 200g
  • Saffron - One pinch
  • Cardamom Powder - 1/4 teaspoon
  • Lemon Juice - 1 teaspoon
  • Plain Flour - 125g
  • Cornflour - 2 tablespoons
  • Natural Yogurt - 2 teaspoons
  • Turmeric - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Ghee - 1 tablespoon
  • Baking Powder - 1/4 teaspoon
  • Bicarbonate of Soda - 1/4 teaspoon

Instructions :

  1. Syrup - Put the sugar and 100 ml water in a pan set over a medium heat. When the sugar has dissolved, add the saffron and bring to the boil
  2. Continue to boil for 5-6 mins until thickened and syrupy – the consistency should be like honey. Remove from the heat and add the cardamom and lemon juice – the lemon juice prevents the sugar crystallising. Set aside.
  3. For the batter, put all the ingredients except the baking powder and bicarb, in a large bowl. Slowly add 150ml water and whisk for a few minutes until smooth, then add the baking powder and bicarb and stir well – it should be as thick as pancake batter, Pour into a squeeze bottle with a narrow tip, or spoon into a piping bag
  4. Carefully squeeze or pipe swirls of the batter directly into the hot oil – do this in batches (you should be able to fry four or five at once). Fry for 1-2 mins on each side until golden.
  5. Remove the jalebi with a slotted spoon to a plate lined with kitchen paper to drain, then, while still warm, dip in the syrup using tongs or a fork, turning to coat. Sprinkle over the flaked almonds or chopped pistachios. Serve straightaway or leave it to cool.
